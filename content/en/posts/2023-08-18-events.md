 ### 37. Mesterségek Ünnepe

The 37th Mesterségek Ünnepe, which translates to "Festival of Crafts," will be held from August 18 to August 20 at the Budai Vár in Polgárváros, Hungary. This event is the largest and most prestigious folk art festival in the Carpathian Basin. Organized by Mesterségek Ünnepe, it brings together artisans, craftsmen, and folk artists to showcase their skills and talents.

The festival will take place in the polgárvárosi rész, which includes Tóth Árpád sétány, Táncsics Mihály and Tárnok utca, Szentháromság tér and park, and Dísz tér. As part of the state celebration, admission to the event is free.

This year, the festival's theme is "Befordultam a konyhára…" or "I turned to the kitchen…," focusing on the traditional peasant cuisine and its utensils that are still relevant today. Visitors can expect to see around a thousand master craftsmen showcasing their skills, along with craft demonstrations and workshops.

One of the highlights of the festival is a fashion show presented by FolkTrend, where traditional clothing is reimagined with a modern twist. There will also be a variety of performances on multiple stages throughout the day, including children's concerts, storytelling sessions, and dance workshops. Visitors can also participate in hands-on craft activities.

The main stage at Szentháromság tér will feature a lineup of performances throughout the festival. On August 18, there will be concerts by Farkas Zoltán Batyu and the Új Gereben band, followed by the Tükrös Együttes. The day will end with a concert by Lakatos Mónika and the Romengo band.

August 19 will feature a special performance by Paár Julcsi, known for her children's songs, followed by collaborations between the ViGaD Zenekar and the Tanac Táncegyüttes. The day will also include concerts by the Tatros Együttes and Tímár Sára, among others.

On August 20, there will be an award ceremony presented by the NESZ (National Cultural and Arts Award) and a concert by the Kármentő Zenekar. The day will also include a bread blessing ceremony with Petrás Mária and a fashion show titled "folkTrend!" featuring modern interpretations of traditional costumes.

Throughout the festival, visitors will have the opportunity to immerse themselves in Hungarian folk art, music, and traditions. The Mesterségek Ünnepe is a celebration of cultural heritage and a testament to the skill and creativity of local artisans.

 ### Aranyvonat kiállítás és Szent Jobb-körmenet

Title: Aranyvonat Exhibition and Szent Jobb Procession: A Celebration of Hungarian Heritage

Description: Don't miss the opportunity to witness one of Hungary's most spectacular national treasures, the Aranyvonat (Golden Train)! This impressive train composition was created in 1938 to commemorate the 900th anniversary of the death of our founding king, Szent István. Unfortunately, during World War II, the railway carriage transporting the holy right hand of Szent István was destroyed.

In 2015, the Budakeszi Cultural Foundation took the initiative to reconstruct the Aranyvonat, and the resurrected train was officially unveiled on August 20, 2020. Now, you have the chance to see this magnificent train up close and celebrate Szent István Nap (Saint Stephen's Day) with your family and friends.

The exhibition and procession will take place from August 18 to August 20. Admission to the event is free, allowing everyone to experience the rich history and cultural heritage of Hungary. The Aranyvonat will transport you back in time, offering a glimpse into the past and the significance of Szent István's legacy.

Come and join us at this extraordinary event as we pay tribute to Szent István, the first king of Hungary, and celebrate the spirit of our nation. Don't miss out on this unique opportunity to be a part of the Aranyvonat Exhibition and Szent Jobb Procession.

For more information about the event, please visit our website: [https://szentistvannap.hu/programok](https://szentistvannap.hu/programok). Let's come together and revel in the beauty of our cultural heritage!
