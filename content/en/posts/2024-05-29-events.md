 ### Florence Vatan: A Concept on the Move: Gestalt in Interwar France

### Florence Vatan: A Concept on the Move: Gestalt in Interwar France

#### Details:
- **Date and Time:** Wednesday, May 29, 2024 at 12:30‚ÄØPM CEST
- **Location:** Not Specified
- **Organizer:** Institute for Advanced Study, CEU

Florence Vatan will delve into the fascinating topic of how concepts evolve and reshape as they traverse geographical and disciplinary borders. Specifically focusing on the early dissemination of Gestalt psychology in France during the 1920s and 1930s, she will explore the intricate transformations these concepts undergo. Through an examination of primary sources such as lectures, translations, publications, and course offerings, the discussion will center on the roles of influential mediators like Paul Guillaume, Ignace Meyerson, and Aron Gurwitsch. Their contributions shed light on the complex dynamics of conceptual reproduction, erasure, and reformation that occur as ideas migrate through various intellectual and disciplinary landscapes.

Get ready to immerse yourself in the rich history of Gestalt psychology and uncover the hidden layers of conceptual evolution. Join Florence Vatan as she unravels the mysteries of how ideas shape-shift and adapt when exposed to new environments and influences. This event promises to be a thought-provoking journey into the realm of intellectual cross-pollination, offering fresh insights into the vibrant tapestry of interwar France. Don't miss out on this unique opportunity to explore the movement of concepts and the profound impact of key mediators in shaping the intellectual landscape of a bygone era.

*Please note that the event timing is in Budapest local time (CEST). Keep your calendars marked for this intriguing exploration into the world of Gestalt psychology in interwar France!*
